Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3D-GS) has recently attracted great attention with real-time and photo-realistic renderings. However, the high-quality of 3D-GS is built upon a great amount of images that require extensive human labor to capture, especially in large-scale scenarios. In this paper, we combine 3D-GS with panoramic vision to introduce PanoGaussian, which reduces data capturing costs by utilizing the broad field of view in panoramic inputs. PanoGaussian takes panoramic images as input and project panoramic regions onto planar images to be compatible with mainstream 3D-GS workflows. Specifically, several most reliable regions are firstly searched based on a texture complexity map of the panoramic image. The reliable regions contain rich textures that facilitate keypoint matching, thus providing accurate pose estimation. Afterwards, Cubemap Projection is conducted on the panoramic image to supplement the areas that are not covered in the reliable regions. The poses of cube regions are derived from the poses of reliable regions. Finally, all the reliable regions as well as cube regions are utilized to reconstruct the 3D-GS model. Moreover, we build a Panoramic Gaussian Splatting dataset called PGS-Data, which consists of both synthetic and real data. Extensive experiments based on several 3D-GS workflows confirm the superiority of PanoGaussian in terms of both rendering quality and efficiency.

Pipeline

The pipeline of PanoGaussian consists of two branches: the reliable region branch (Br) and the cube region branch (Bc). Br generates texture complexity map from the input panoramic image and picks several reliable regions based on tile-based calculation, then estimates the poses of the reliable regions. Bc generates the cube regions to supplement the uncovered areas and calculates the poses of the cube regions based on the poses of the reliable regions. Finally, all the reliable regions as well as cube regions are utilized to reconstruct the 3D-GS model.

Related Links

In FastScene[Ma et al., 2024], researchers introduced a novel panoramic view synthesis method PNVI, becoming the first to solve panoramic 3DGS from a single panorama.

SceneDreamer360[Li et al., 2024] proposed a novel SceneDreamer360 for text-driven 3D-consistent scene generation using panoramic Gaussian splatting, resulting in detailed scene content and spatial consistency.

360-GS[Bai et al., 2024] proposed a layout-guided 3D Gaussian splatting pipeline and a high-quality point cloud generation method for the initialization of 3D Gaussians from room layout to improve the performance of few-shot novel view synthesis.
